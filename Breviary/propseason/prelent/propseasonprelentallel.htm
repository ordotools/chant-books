The Afternoon of Saturday
before Septuagesima

Commonly called Alleluia Saturday

Vespers

On the morrow, being a II Class Sunday, no Feast may be kept. A II Class Double occuring tomorrow is transferred, and other Feasts have a Commemoration, according to the Rubrics.

If today is a I or II Class double, Vespers is II Vespers of that feast with a Commemoration of Saturday Vespers.

Otherwise it is as follows:

Psalms with their Antiphons from the Psalter, and then:

Capitulum           1 Cor. 9. 24.
Fratres: Nescítis quod ii qui in stádio currunt, omnes quidem currunt, sed unus áccipit bravíum?  Sic cúrrite ut comprehendátis.
R.  Deo grátias.

The Little Chapter        1 Cor. 9. 24.
Brethren: Know ye not that those who run in a race, all indeed run, but only one receiveth the prize?  Run ye so as to obtain it.
R.  Thanks be to God.

MAGNIFICAT THE MAGNIFICAT

Oremus.
Preces pópuli tui, quæsumus, Dómine, cleménter exáudi: ut, qui juste pro peccátis nostris afflígimur, pro tui nóminis glória misericórditer  liberémur.  Per Dóminum.

Let us pray.
O Lord, we beseech thee favourably to hear the prayers of thy people: that we, who are justly punished for our offences, may be mercifully delivered by thy goodness, for the glory of thy Name.  Through.
The anticipated Sunday after Epiphany is not commemorated here.

V.  May the souls † of the faithful departed, through the mercy of God, rest in peace.
R.  Amen.

Et sic dicitur præcedens Versus hoc Sabbato, etiam si Vesperæ fuerint de Festo duplici I vel II classis.

Et deinceps non dicitur Allelúja, usque ad Sabbatum sanctum. Sed post Deus, in adjutórium, ubi dicitur Allelúja, dicitur Laus tibi, Dómine, Rex ætérnæ glóriæ.

The Closing Versicles are thus said at Vespers on this Saturday, whether the Office be of Saturday or of a I or II Class Double, in which case all is of the Feast instead of the Saturday.

And henceforward Alleluia is not said anywhere in the Office until Holy Saturday. Rather, after O God, make speed to save me, where before Alleluja was said, there is now said, All praise be unto thee, O Lord, King of glory everlasting.

Then is said secretly the final Pater, unless Compline immediately follow; in which case the Triple or Dual Prayer thereof, as the case may be, is immediately said, followed by the Opening Versicles, as is prescribed in Common Forms.
