The body without the soul could not conceive desire.  The body's power to enjoy cometh from the same source as its power to choose.  Hence it is the duty of the soul to deny something to its subject, (to wit, to the lower nature,) and thereby keep back the outer man from things unseemly.  Then will the soul be less hindered by fleshly cravings, and have leisure in the inner court of the mind to dwell on the wisdom of God.  There, when the turmoil of earthly care is stilled, will the soul feed on holy thoughts, and be entertained with the expectation of everlasting joy.

In the ferial Office in Advent
Until December 23rd inclusive

In each of the hours, the various parts are proper to Advent, as given in the appropriate place.

In Matins on Wednesday, the psalms are taken from the second place in the third nocturn.

In Lauds, the psalms are taken from Lauds 2. The antiphons in the psalter are used until December 16th inclusive, but from December 17th to the 23rd inclusive there are proper antiphons, as given in the appropriate place.

In the small hours, the antiphons are the usual ones through December 16th inclusive, after which they are proper, as noted in the appropriate place. The fourth psalm is said in Prime, and the versicle in Prime is proper as well.

In Vespers, the Antiphons and psalms are from the psalter; the other parts are proper to Advent, as noted in the appropriate place.

The Preces Feriales are said in all the hours.
