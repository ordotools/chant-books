The rubric for the anticipated Sunday after Epiphany is placed at the end of Friday of the First Week after the Epiphany, and reads as follows:

Si sequens Dominica II vel alia post Epiphaniam, Septuagesima superveniente impediatur, nec sit ei locus post Pentecosten, juxta Rubricas, de ea anticipatur Officium Sabbato sub ritu semiduplici, cum omnibus privilegiis Dominicæ propriis, tam in occursu, quam in concursu ad I Vesperas. In II autem Vesperis nihil fit de ea, quamvis sequens Dominica Septuagesimæ commemoranda tantum sit, juxta Rubricas.

In Officio hujus Dominicæ anticipatæ omnia dicuntur de Sabbato, et, in I Vesperis et Completorio eas sequenti, die Feria VI, ut in Psalterio, præter Capitulum Primæ, quod dicitur de Dominica, ut in Ordinario, Orationem, Lectiones cum suis Responsoriis, et Antiphonam ad Benedictus, quæ de ipsa Dominica dicuntur. In I autem Nocturno ponuntur Lectiones de Initio Epistolarum beati Pauli, quæ in Dominica anticipata et duabus etiam ex sequentibus Feriis occurrant; et Initia Epistolarum sequentibus Feriis et Dominicis assignata, penitus omittuntur. Initium vero Epistolæ quod assignatur Sabbato, in quod anticipatur Officium Dominicæ ut supra, ponitur in Feria proxime antecedenti, jux Rubricas.

If the following Second Sunday or any other Sunday after the Epiphany is impeded by Septuagesima, and there is no place for it after Pentecost, according to the Rubrics, its Office is anticipated on the Saturday, under the rite of a Semidouble, with all the privileges proper to the Sunday, both in occurrence and in concurrence at First Vespers.  But at Second Vespers, no notice is taken of it, even if the following Septuagesima Sunday has only to be commemorated, according to the Rubrics.

In Vespers and Compline of Friday before this anticipated Sunday, everything is said as in the Psalter, and likewise on Saturday, except the Little Chapter of Prime, which is taken from the Sunday, as given in the Ordinary. Also, the collect, the Lessons with their Responsories, and the Antiphon at the Benedictus, are taken from the Sunday itself. But at the I Nocturn, the Lessons are taken from the beginning of the Epistles of St. Paul which occur on the anticipated Sunday, and even from the two following Ferias; and the beginnings of the Epistles assigned for the following Ferias and Sundays are wholly omitted.  But the beginning of the Epistle which is assigned to the Saturday on which the Office of the Sunday is anticipated, as above, is said on the Feria immediately before, according to the Rubrics.

Rubrical note: In February of 2020 the ordo said the antiphon was Suscepit. After reading the rubric for the anticipated Sunday, given at the top of this page, I concluded that, as it says, everything is said as given in the psalter except four things:

1. The Chapter of Prime

2. The Collect

3. The lessons with their responsories,

4. The antiphon at the Benedictus.

Everything else is taken from the psalter, according to the rubric. Now, the antiphon in vespers is not one of the four exceptions to the rule given, nor is it found in the psalter, so it is not said. In its place is said the antiphon that is given in the psalter, that is, the ferial antiphon.

Fr. Lehtoranta objected that the feast is of semi-double rite, and this is first vespers. Against this, it can be said:

1. The rubric and its application is clear. It does not indicate Suscepit, but by elimination only leaves the ferial rubric.

2. The other parts of this office are of the feria, such as the invitatorium, hymn of Matins, chapters of the small hours, etc. There is nothing against having the antiphon of the Magnificat also be of the feria.

3. In any case, if you read the rubric, the algorithm seems to be to take everything from the feria except for the proper elements that the Sunday has. So, since the Sundays seem to have a proper Magnificat antiphon, it follows that that also is taken from the Sunday. However, although most Sundays during the year have proper antiphons for their 1st vespers, the Sundays after Epiphany do not. Rather, they all share the antiphon Suscepit. So consistently with the way the rubric works, you do not take the Magnificat antiphon from the Sunday because it does not have its own Magnificat antiphon. It only has the Suscepit antiphon, which has been said several times already.

This is furthermore confirmed, yet again, by reference to any of the 8vo Pustet breviaries that do not have the Chapter and Hymn in the psalter in vespers.
