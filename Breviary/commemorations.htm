Commemorations in Lauds and Vespers are observed in the following order:

1. The commemorations which belong to an office, e.g. the commemoration of St. Paul on the feast of St. Peter.

2. The office of the following day, or of the preceding day, as the case may be.

3. A Sunday or the vigil of Epiphany.

4. A day within the octave of Epiphany or Corpus Christi.

5. An octave day which has the rank of Double Major.

6. A Double Major.

7. A Double Minor (otherwise known as a Double)

8. A Semidouble.

9. A day within the octave of Christmas, the Ascension or the Sacred Heart.

10. A day within a common octave.

11. The Friday after the octave of the Ascension.

12. A ferial day in Lent or Advent, Rogation Monday, or the Ember Days in September.

13. A vigil.

14. The octave day of a simple octave. (The feasts with simple octaves are those of the Nativity of Our Lady, St. John the Apostle, St. Stephen, the Holy Innocents, and St. Lawrence.)

15. A feast of simple rank.
